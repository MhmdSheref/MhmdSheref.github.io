Patch 1.0: Industrial Revolution Carbon emissions from burning fossil fuels began to increase significantly, leading to a steady rise in global temperatures. Patch 2.0: The Greenhouse Effect Scientists discover that certain gases in the atmosphere, such as carbon dioxide, trap heat from the sun and contribute to global warming. Patch 3.0: Climate Change The term "climate change" is coined to encompass not only rising temperatures, but also the wide-ranging impacts of global warming, including changes in precipitation, sea level, and extreme weather events. Patch 4.0: Paris Agreement The international community comes together to set ambitious goals for reducing carbon emissions and slowing the pace of climate change. Patch 5.0: Climate Emergency The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) issues dire warnings about the urgent need to take action to avoid the worst effects of climate change. Patch 6.0: Renewable Energy Advances in renewable energy technology make it increasingly cost-effective to shift away from fossil fuels and reduce carbon emissions. Patch 7.0: Carbon Pricing Countries and cities begin to implement carbon pricing mechanisms, such as carbon taxes and cap-and-trade systems, to put a price on carbon emissions and incentivize reductions. Patch 8.0: Climate Adaptation Communities and governments around the world begin to take steps to adapt to the unavoidable impacts of climate change, such as sea level rise and increased frequency of extreme weather events. Patch 9.0: The Future The future of our planet and the fate of humanity are in our hands. It's up to us to take action now and continue to make updates and progress in the fight against global warming and climate change.
